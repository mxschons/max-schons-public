## What's the blood pressure device you have? (2022-07)

[Aktiia](https://aktiia.com/). Continuous blood pressure device with a dedicated app, licensed as a medical device. I've used it for multiple months (with a long break when mine broke). Works great. One catch: the biggest changes in my average blood pressure came from recalibrating the device.

## What is the portable laptop stand you use? (2022-07)

The [Nexstand K2](https://nexstand.eu/products/nexstand-k2-laptop-stand-1). Robust, adjustable height while the laptop is on it. Probably my single most useful computer gadget — prevents headaches from looking down at the screen.

I've tried [one other stand](https://www.therooststand.com/) which was thinner but didn't have in-use height adjustment.

## What is the value proposition of co-working? (2022-07)

- **Taking breaks instead of working for hours in a row.** Co-working built my habit of actually taking breaks. A break is not turning off your head and going into social-media mode — it's stepping back from work, letting your brain into free association, adding movement / exercise, and noticing needs like water, rest, hygiene.
- **Interactions with colleagues across teams.** Easy socializing; a sense of what others are working on. Good way to break out of team / satellite isolation.
- **Outside perspective.** Colleagues give invaluable outside takes and immediate coaching / mentorship during calls.
- **Accountability / motivation to start working.** You're accountable to show up. Can be a single call in the morning or afternoon at the time you find hardest to get started on important work.
- **Structured day.** Scoped work sessions instead of a stream of tasks.

## How does Max organize his contacts? (2022-12)

I use [Dex](https://getdex.com/) as my contact management tool. Not perfect, but the best I've found.

The good:
- Import from many sources — easy for LinkedIn and Google accounts; Swapcard works if you convert the Excel export to CSV.
- Nice interface and shortcuts.
- Simple filters, saveable as views.
- Contact-rhythms sorting.
- Easy export of everything.
- Email forwarding lets you create contacts easily.

To optimize:
- Merge functionality doesn't catch all duplicates.
- No two-way sync with Apple / Google Contacts, and no integration with Apple Mail / Gmail.

I imported ~850 contacts, cleaned them up, and sorted them into groups / contact rhythms. Took ~5 hours. Most well-organized contacts list I've ever had.

| Lipid Panel | Total cholesterol, LDL, HDL, triglycerides, oxidized LDL, HS-OMEGA index | LDL < 70 |

| hs-CRP | | |
| Holotranscobalamin | B12 marker | Standard lab ranges |
| Cystatin C | Best kidney marker | |
| Hormone panel | Total testosterone, SHBG, free androgen index, Vermeulen free testosterone (get 2–3 fasting measurements), LH, FSH, estradiol, prolactin | |
| Vitamin D | Important for many things | > 50 ng/mL |
| Continuous Glucose Monitor (every 2–3y) | For 2–8 weeks | Average < 100, SD < 15 |

**OGTT reference ranges (per Peter Attia):**

- Glucose: fasting 60–100 mg/dL (Peter likes < 90); 1h < 200 (Peter likes < 120–130, depending on muscle mass); 2h < 140 (Peter likes < 100). You can be in these ranges and still have postprandial hyperinsulinemia.
- Insulin: fasting < 25 mIU/L (Peter likes < 6); 1h 18–276 (Peter likes < 30); 2h 16–166 (Peter likes < 20).

Peter Attia's full panel with optimal / low / high columns is in his [AMA series](https://peterattiamd.com/ama01/).

### Physical testing

Most of these should be done roughly yearly (6–24 months).

| Value | Description | Reference range |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|
| Blood pressure | Standard medical protocol for 2 weeks morning + evening. Every ~2y a 24h measurement. | < 120/80 |
| Resting heart rate | Any wearable | < 60 |
| Sleep lab | [Home device](https://amzn.eu/d/aD6XCPw) for the 6–24 month cadence. Every ~5y, proper sleep-lab gear with expert physicians. | Up to 5 events is "normal", but I want 0–1 hypoxia and 0 apnoea events per hour. |
| Skill assessment | 4 things at every joint (ankle, knee, hip): symmetry, stability, slow controlled movement, awareness (e.g. heels down when squatting), full range of motion. | Experienced trainer testing you, or a video review (tight clothes). |
| VO2 max | Wearables approximate (underestimate); proper test is better. | Up to 40y: > 50 (males and females) |
| Grip strength | [Dynamometer](https://amzn.eu/d/7j6fdTX), age-adjusted > 90th percentile. For a 30y male ~65kg. Little left / right difference. | 2-min dead hang + 1-min farmer carry with bodyweight is excellent. |
| Strength (lower body) | Squat with dumbbell | Hold ~half bodyweight at bottom for ~45s |
| DXA full-body scan | Follow up with separate bone-density scan if needed | ALMI 9–10 males / 7–8 females; FFMI > 22 males / 18.5 females |
| Muscular endurance | Push-up with full elbow lockout at top + chest touch at bottom | 25 consecutive (male) / 15 (female) |
| Power tests | Standing jump; vertical jump both hands | >> your body size; > 60cm male / > 50cm female (age-adjusted: > 50y → > 50cm male, > 42cm female) |
| Anaerobic capacity | Heart-rate recovery at max / at peak fatigue | 30 bpm recovery in 60s (~½ beat per second) |
| Brain MRI | (Want to explore more in the future.) | |

### Lead measures

Weekly inputs for optimal health:

- 3h low-effort cardio (60–80% max HR)
- 1h high-effort cardio (> 80% max HR with breaks)
  - Most established regimen: 4 min at 90–95% max HR + 4 min "rest" at 70–80% HR ([Helgerud et al. 2007](https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0113637))
- 3h strength training
- 1h stability / injury prevention / stretching
- One rest week every 3–6 months

Sleep references: [JAMA](https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jama/fullarticle/2798400), [NEJM](https://www.nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJMcp1816152), [Huberman on sleep (YouTube)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WkPjOmgh40A).

## Finance procedures I find valuable (2023-06)

> The budget categories below are ones I came up with for myself. Adapt them to your own life.

### Ops

- One separate credit card for tax-deductible payments (easy to find at year end).
- Weekly oversight across all accounts. I use [finanzblick](https://finanzblickx.buhl.de/login), which also lets you upload / forward invoices straight into your tax returns. I check for tax-relevant payments, weird payments, and tag things.
  - Sub-account for money I've lent to others or owe to others.
- Be smart about what you can declare for taxes — can easily save thousands (education invoices, work-related travel, etc.).
- Pay everything by card where you can, for easier oversight.
