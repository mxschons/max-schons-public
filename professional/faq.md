## Communication preferences

**TLDR:** Assume I haven't read most messages in channels. If you need me to read something quickly, DM me.

Slack and email grow beyond my capacity to keep up with — there are too many initiatives and threads. In the spirit of my well-being, I don't want to feel bad for not processing everything when the task is close to impossible.

What I reliably read, in order:

- **Slack**:
  - DMs
  - Threads I've been involved in (until I explicitly unsubscribe)
  - A small set of most-important channels
  - Temporary most-important channels
- **Email**: everything, but when I'm CC'd expect me to skim, not read.

I try to read Slack threads I've been tagged in, but expect delays. If you really want me to read something quickly, DM me a link to the message.

Heuristic: if a message isn't in an actively monitored channel and I haven't been tagged, expect I haven't read it.

## Max' calendar setup (2025-04)

1. Use [Reclaim.ai](https://reclaim.ai/) to sync personal calendar to professional and vice versa. Professional tiers allow syncing multiple calendars.
2. Use OOO events to signal when you're usually blocked. Easily moved if timezones change; provides buffer.
3. When you want to get something done, schedule it in your calendar — ideally invite someone else to the event.
4. Strongly recommend:
   - At least one meeting-free day every week. Add as a blocker so others can see it.
   - A private weekly check-in on Monday morning to orient yourself.
   - A personal check-in for life-admin and non-work stuff on Friday morning, so you can act during usual office hours.

## How do I think about different roles in an org? (2022-08)

### The roles and responsibilities in a squad

| Role | Area of responsibility | Held accountable by |
|------|------------------------|---------------------|
| **Patron** ("the supporter / executive") | Enablement, holding accountable, and verification of results of the Owner. Not their responsibility to determine *how* the mission is achieved. | None (accountability via higher management / people informed). |
| **Orchestrator / Owner** ("the team lead") | Accountability for success of the (sub-)mission. Authority to orchestrate team members, assign tasks, enable people. Backup / default for executing any (group of) tasks. Keeps people tagged as informed up to date. Writes post-action reports on the mission. | Patron. |
| **Executer** ("team member") | Accountability for executing the (sub-)task. Authority to make decisions in the scope of the (sub-)task. Execution of delegated tasks. Proactively engages with consultants. | Owner. |
| **Consultant** | Provides high-quality input and recommendations on questions raised by any team member. Double-checks recommendations. | Executer / Owner. |
| **Informed / Informer** | Acknowledges information, incorporates it into mental models, proactively shares info from outside domains that could influence decision-making. | Patron. |

## My management template (2023-04)

A check-in template I use in 1-1s with people I manage (Week XX):

1. Brag about at least one professional success of the past week.
2. Current job satisfaction (-10 to 10). Three most relevant variables influencing this right now?
3. What are you procrastinating these days?
4. What level are your stress metrics at?
5. What keeps you away from doing your best work right now? What should you say no to?
6. Who can help you get unstuck on any of the above?
7. Action points of the last meeting(s).
8. Reflect on at least one mistake / challenge over the past week:
   - What exactly happened?
   - Have I made mistakes like this before?
   - What was the immediate cause?
   - What was the root cause? Bonus: is there a function of the root behavior I should be aware of?
   - How can / did I resolve the immediate cause?
   - How can / did I resolve the root cause?
9. What risks and issues do you anticipate for your work and / or performance over the next 1–2 weeks?
   - Risk + mitigation strategy.
   - Do you anticipate personal performance risks / issues?
10. What risks are you currently averse to that could yield unusually high results?
11. Braindump — what's on your mind (questions, asks, tasks, ughs, …)?
    - Self-care (solo time, movement + exercise, life admin, social).
12. What deliberate growth activities & professional-excellence training are you engaged in (maybe KPIs)?
13. Outlook:
    - Immediate (helpful) next steps after this session (what and how)?
    - What should we focus on next time?
    - Was this session helpful (scale -10 to 10)?

**For me:** Anything I want to give feedback on, especially performance risks / issues?

### Other prompts

- What are we / you neglecting right now that could cause serious trouble in the future?
- What are we / you fooling ourselves about right now?
- Who could I be asking for help?
- What should I parallelize?

### Ownership questions

Ownership — taken seriously — means you:

- Actively seek responsibility for results.
- Deliver results, and if mission-critical, do close to everything imaginable to prevent failure.
- Demand and foster authority to achieve results.
- Clarify, monitor, and verify the interplay of other agents.
- Own full accountability for the consequences of your work product, even when upstream / downstream others failed to deliver.

Reflection prompts:

- Where do you work on a *process* rather than a *result*?
- Where and how could you increase your clarity on responsibilities?
- Where should you demand more / less or foster authority?
- Where do you weasel away from consequences of your work?

> "What's one decision you can make today that will save you from making more decisions in the future? Make the choice that eliminates other choices."

- What can you delegate completely?
- What can you automate completely?
- What can you meaningfully simplify?

- Who should I do a co-working session with this week?
- What fun things can I share with people in the org?
- Am I being open about being overwhelmed / stressed / low energy via meta-schedules?

## How to best run an all-hands (2023-05)

Four core functions of an all-hands. Independent of content, all-hands ought to be interesting, digestible, entertaining / propulsive.

| Function | Definition: every attendee leaves all-hands… | Form (tactics) |
|----------|----------------------------------------------|----------------|
| **Clarity & alignment** | …with a better understanding and ability to articulate where we're coming from, where we are now, and where we're going. | Goal updates; essential new processes / structures; presenting the roadmap; a from-to for the past week and a from-to for the next week; AMA / Q&A feature. |
| **Enhancement** | …clearer on what defines our way of working together, and better at carrying / contributing to / improving it. | Deep dive + training on a topic (values, mental models, communication, mission). Direct role-modeling of culture during the meeting (speaking about challenging feedback, culture wins, operational excellence). Challenge of the week. Thanks-for-the-feedback. Live feedback at the end (plus / ∆). |
| **Sense of purpose** | …with refreshed excitement about the mission; feels the relevance of achievements and takes pride in how their and colleagues' work contributes. | Celebrate exceptional work outputs publicly (win of the week). Explicitly connect work outputs to mission. Collaborate and co-create something (redteaming session, live polling) so people feel they have a say. |
| **Connection** | …feeling more part of a group they care about. | Breakout rooms for synchronous peer conversation. Pull up something personal (music, picture) before joining. Shout-out appreciations. |

### Default agenda

Covers all four core functions. An all-hands can also be weighted toward one of the four. Aim for max 50 min; ideal 45 min so people can recover.

**Clarity & alignment — 10 min**
- 1 min: welcome, glance over agenda
- 2 × 2 min: announcements (prep: all-hands ops asks all coordinators / department leads for announcements). Must be relevant to the whole org, not only a subset; happened last week or certainly happening next week.
- 5 min: goal update(s) (prep: goal owner)

**Connection — 10 min**
- 2 min: shout-out appreciations
- 8 min (5–10): breakout room (prep: all-hands ops provides prompts; coordinator may hijack for next section)

**Enhancement + sense of purpose — 20 min** (prep: coordinator)
- Changing content each week.

**Outro — 5 min**
- 2 min: TFTF (prep: all-hands ops finds a person)
- 2 min: live feedback for this all-hands
- 1 min: wrap up

**Buffer (or end early!) — 5 min**

## How I prepare feedback for reports (2023-05)

I interview 4–5 people using questions like the ones below and summarize the output in a 1–2 page document for the individual.

Ground rules for interviewees:

- This is anonymous.
- This is not a people-pleasing exercise — practice radical candor.
- If you're uncomfortable sharing something, flag it so I can obfuscate it more.

### Core questions

- What behavioral patterns come up when you think about XXX?
- Where is XXX a role model for you?
- On what performance percentile is XXX from your perspective within the organization overall?
- How likely are you to recommend this colleague to another suitable team or company? (0–10)
- The most significant thing that holds XXX back from making their biggest contribution is…
- Open feedback on the below:

### Dimensions to probe

**Ownership.** Actively seeks responsibility for results. Delivers results (mission-critical → does close to everything imaginable to prevent failure). Demands and fosters authority to achieve results. Clarifies, monitors, and verifies the interplay of other agents — do they catch mistakes before they cause trouble? Seeks risky opportunities and minimizes downside risk. Owns full accountability for consequences, even when upstream / downstream others failed to deliver.

**Accountability.** Proactively writes post-action reports. Identifies core process changes to prevent problems from recurring. Implements visible process changes immediately. Ideally trains or simulates the new process. Publicly communicates they're taking accountability for the mistake and shares the above. Revisits the reflection regularly.

**Communication.**

**Pragmatism.**

### Context-builder questions

- Concrete examples of excellence, average work, poor performance?
- Does XXX ever make you uncomfortable — if so, how? Does XXX ever make you comfortable?
- Is XXX a great team player? What are they contributing to the team?
- Who should XXX talk to to learn more about a specific ability / character trait? Who should mentor XXX?
- How would you describe XXX's feedback culture?
- Where do you observe XXX deliberately practicing a skill / development area? Do you see it at all?
- What could be a professional goal for next quarter for XXX? What should XXX practice deliberately?

## My OOO checklist

For OOO situations ≥ 2 business days.

### 1 week prior
- Block time for catching up when back (heuristic: 1h per day OOO).
- Ask people to share their open questions for me before I leave.
- Block time for OOO prep the day before I'm actually OOO.

### If longer OOO
- Look ahead 4 weeks and plan what needs to get initiated.

### 1 day prior
- Set up OOO email auto-reply.
- Set up OOO Slack status.
- Post on Slack that I'm OOO now.

### Availability during OOO
- When I'm OOO I am **not available** for any work-related activities unless explicitly agreed beforehand. I don't check Slack or email and don't attend meetings.
- Emergencies ("Max is literally the only one with a password for this and we need it now") are the only reason to contact me. A senior team lead must explicitly agree it's an emergency and approve contacting me.

### Backups
- Responsibilities and quarterly planning tracked by AOR in a shared sheet.

### Unexpectedly OOO
- Coordinators have access to my full todo list and notes from past conversations I haven't parsed yet.
- An executive assistant should have access to my email; worst case, a designated coordinator can reset my password and give someone access.
- External meetings can be found via the calendar or listed in SavvyCal.

### Sample OOO email

> Hello,
>
> I will be away from the office until **[DATE]** with no access to email. If your request is urgent, please contact **[NAME]** at **[CONTACT]**.

## Guiding prompts for executives (2023-04)

1. Your most important job is making sure your team is kicking ass. What can this group do to make that more likely and consistent?
2. Where is clarity lacking for you on what we're doing, why, and how it's going (at org-wide level)? (Assume any ambiguity you experience is compounded for your people by 5–10×.)
3. Get on the balcony: what is a challenge or problem you foresee that we can either (a) quickly solve here together, or (b) delegate to someone (in this group or on the team)?
