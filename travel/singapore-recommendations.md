# Singapore Recommendations

## Logistics

- **[Grab](https://www.grab.com/sg/)** is the default for taxis and food delivery. You can top up GrabPay and use it as a wallet in some stores.
- Carry some cash. A number of hawker stalls only accept cash or local payment methods (PayNow, GrabPay).
- Public transport is trivial. Just tap your contactless credit card at the gate. No need for a separate transit card.
- Singapore is extremely safe. It is normal to leave your laptop at a Starbucks while you go to the bathroom, and nobody will touch it.
- Singapore enforces its drug laws very strictly, including the death penalty for trafficking above specific thresholds. Executions have been in the mid-teens per year recently (17 in 2025, a 22-year high). Do not take any chances with illicit substances.
- Keep an umbrella and slippers handy. Tropical rain arrives without warning and ends just as quickly.

## Food

- **Hawker centres** are where you eat. The best ones in or near the city centre are in [Chinatown (Chinatown Complex, Maxwell)](https://www.visitsingapore.com/dining-drinks-singapore/local-dishes/hawker-food-guide/), [Lau Pa Sat](https://www.laupasat.sg/) on Market Street, and Amoy Street Food Centre. Bring cash or GrabPay.
- **[Haidilao Hotpot](https://www.haidilao.com/sg/)**. If you have not done Haidilao elsewhere, Singapore has many locations and the experience is excellent. Good vegetarian broths and sides.
- **Mala xiang guo**. Look for a store where you pick your own vegetables and protein from a display and they stir fry it fresh with chili and spices. Common in hawker centres and food courts.

## Getting oriented

The [hop-on hop-off bus](https://www.bigbustours.com/en/singapore/singapore-bus-tours/) is genuinely useful on day one. It gives you a solid feel for the layout and makes it easy to combine two areas in a single day.

## Marina Bay Sands area

- Walk from **[Clarke Quay](https://www.clarkequay.com.sg/)** (or further up the river) down to MBS along the water. The stretch is pleasant, especially late afternoon.
- **[CÉ LA VI](https://sg.celavi.com/)** on top of MBS. Rooftop bar and club with one of the best views in the city. Best around sunset. There is a cover charge (~SGD 38 including one drink), and reserved tables have minimum spends. Walk-ins are fine for general entry at the bar.
- **[WeWork Battery Road](https://www.wework.com/buildings/9-battery-road--singapore--SG)** at 9 Battery Road (not the other WeWork on the same street, which has a more obvious entrance). Great view of MBS with a terrace. Ideal if you want a few hours of work in a good environment.
- **[Gardens by the Bay](https://www.gardensbythebay.com.sg/)** is right next to MBS. The Supertree Grove light show (free, evenings) and the two conservatories (Cloud Forest, Flower Dome) are worth it.

## Orchard area

- Very touristy, but worth a visit.
- **[Trifecta](https://trifectasingapore.com/)** next to Somerset MRT has Asia's largest indoor surfing wave pool, plus indoor snowboarding and a skate bowl. Unique in the region.
- **[Ion Sky](https://www.ionorchard.com/en/experience/ion-sky.html)** at ION Orchard. Free observation deck on the 56th floor with a wide view over the city.

## Chinatown and central area

- **Chinatown Complex Food Centre** and **Maxwell Food Centre** are prime hawker spots. GrabPay or cash for most stalls.
- Chinatown has a lot of cheap and good massage places. Walk around and pick one that looks clean and busy.

## Parks and nature

- **[Singapore Botanic Gardens](https://www.nparks.gov.sg/sbg)** (UNESCO site). Huge, free, and a great place to decompress. Different from Gardens by the Bay, worth doing both.

## Other worth doing

- **[Mustafa Centre](https://www.mustafa.com.sg/)** in Little India. A 24-hour multi-floor department store that sells everything imaginable. An experience in itself.
- **[VivoCity](https://www.vivocity.com.sg/)** is a massive mall with an excellent food court. Good rainy-day plan.
- **[SEA Aquarium](https://www.rwsentosa.com/en/attractions/sea-aquarium)** on Sentosa. Solid, and easy to combine with VivoCity since they are a short walk apart.
- **[Singapore Zoo and Night Safari](https://www.mandai.com/)**. Nicely done if you enjoy zoos. Skip if you do not.
- **[Changi Airport](https://www.changiairport.com/)** is worth exploring properly on arrival or departure. The Jewel (rain vortex, canopy park), the butterfly garden in T3, and the free city tour for transit passengers are all worth the time.

## Day trip (2 days)

- **[Telunas Private Island](https://telunasresorts.com/)** in Indonesia. Roughly 2.5 hours via ferry from HarbourFront to Sekupang (Batam), then a private boat to the island. Requires an Indonesian e-visa, easy to get [online](https://molina.imigrasi.go.id/). The closest thing to paradise I have come across. Overwater villas, quiet, no Wi-Fi to speak of.

## A local habit worth copying

Spend a slow afternoon at a Starbucks (Clarke Quay or Chinatown are good choices). You will see it packed with students and locals working, studying, and hanging out for hours. It is a very Singapore scene.
